Information

What are you?

- Digital Natives(grew up with today’s technology)
- Digital Immigrants(all the rest of us)
- Digital Strangers (in the workforce before computers; view technology with some suspicion, discomfort and perhaps as a threat)

Digital Natives

  • are multi-taskers
  • graphics before text
  • instant gratification

Digital Immigrants

  • can adapt, but with varying success
  • value the "old way"
  • may be biased against the "Native" ways

Prensky, M. (2001) Digital natives, digital immigrants. On the Horizon, (9)5.
Digital Strangers(in the workforce before computers; view technology with some suspicion, discomfort and perhaps as a threat)

Gotta, M. (2006) Social Computing: Lifestyle Becomes A Workstyle

Implications for Higher Education

  1. Infrastructure and pedagogoy built by/for the immigrants
  2. Adaptation/assimilation is critical
  3. Natives unlikely to "revert" to immigrants standards
